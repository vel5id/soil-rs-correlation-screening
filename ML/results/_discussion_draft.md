### Mechanistic interpretation: why features are locally informative, regional-only, unstable, or uninformative

Decomposing each pooled rank correlation into a between-block (regional) and within-block (local) component, and tagging every feature x target pair for cross-year sign stability, sorts the 3072 pairs into four mechanistically distinct classes: generalizable (96; 3.1%), zonal_only (245; 8.0%), unstable (404; 13.2%), and weak (2327; 75.7%). The taxonomy ranks not correlation strength but *what the correlation means*. Read against the pedology of the chernozem-to-kastanozem steppe and the spatially disjoint two-year design (2022: 174 samples/5 farms; 2023: 911/15 farms, so year is confounded with location), the classes separate genuine soil-physics signal from regional pseudoreplication and small-sample artefacts.

**Generalizable: soil chromophores in spring bare soil.** Only 31 pairs are *robust* (within-sign equals pooled sign, |within| >= 0.17), and these are overwhelmingly spring spectral bands: 26/31 are spring and 17/31 are base Sentinel-2/Landsat-8 reflectance. Because ~75% of samples were taken in spring (Mar-May) over near-bare steppe, a spring image observes the surface in nearly the state the lab sample was drawn, and soil chromophores drive reflectance directly: carbonate and clay-OH overtones in SWIR (B11 ~1.6 um, B12 ~2.2 um), Fe-oxide and organic-matter darkening in VIS-NIR. These chromophores index the same pedogenic factors that set the *stable* properties, so the mapping survives spatial-block detrending and is year-stable because soil mineralogy does not change between campaigns. The anchor is pH ~ l8_GNDVI_spring (within = -0.323, between = -0.703; 22 = -0.294, 23 = -0.615); for exchangeable K2O the robust signals are spring bare-soil/SWIR indices (s2_BSI_spring within = -0.271). GLCM texture also generalizes for pH/P2O5/K2O, because tillage and residue micro-patterning are management-stable across years and computed from within-field neighbourhoods. The per-target generalizable counts (NO3 44, K2O 27, pH 13, P2O5 8, SOC 4, S 0) foreshadow that the labile properties behave differently.

NO3's many hits are not soil sensing but a crop-N proxy. Nitrate has no diagnostic absorption, and its signature is a Simpson-type reversal in which growing-season vegetation-index means correlate *positively* within fields but *negatively* across the region (ts_s2_GNDVI_mean: within = +0.283, between = -0.452, full = -0.236). Two opposing couplings superimpose: within a field, greener patches are the better-fertilized, higher-mineralization patches (more residual N), whereas across the climate gradient the more productive zones have more fully taken up and leached their nitrate (less residual N). The index proxies the crop's own N status -- a mobile nutrient sensed through the canopy, not the soil -- so none of these temporal-mean rows are robust, and the same GNDVI_spring features are merely zonal_only for pH (within ~ 0). Sulfur is the opposite extreme: the only property with *zero* generalizable and zero robust features, because plant-available sulfate has no diagnostic absorption across 400-2500 nm (Viscarra Rossel et al. 2006). Every S correlation is borrowed -- a regional deposition/intensity co-gradient (zonal_only, S greenness indices at between ~ 0.6 but within ~ 0.07-0.12) or a year-specific surface-state confound (unstable). This is a hard sensor-physics limit: adding SWIR or hyperspectral bands cannot create a stable local S signal.

**Unstable: the disjoint-year design, not real change.** The decisive evidence is that physically *time-invariant* covariates flip sign: SOC ~ topo_slope reverses from +0.663 (2022) to -0.481 (2023) although slope and decadal SOC are essentially fixed over one year (similar flips for aspect, MAP, and 9-km ERA5 climate). A reversal in a static feature cannot be temporal; it arises because the disjoint farm sets occupy different limbs of the regional gradient -- a year x location Simpson's paradox. Statistically the class is the zonal_only signature with an unreplicated regional arm: 64% of rows have |within| < 0.10 (mean |within| = 0.098), so the flips ride on a near-absent local signal, and with ~5 effectively independent farms in 2022 the small-n correlation is winner's-cursed and regresses past zero on the cleaner 2023 leg. A genuine temporal component exists only for the labile NO3 summer-VI family (a real positive within-field coupling competing with a negative regional one), but it is perfectly aliased with the spatial confound and cannot be separated here.

**Zonal_only: pseudoreplication.** This class is regional gradient and nothing else: mean |between| = 0.493 against mean |within| = 0.058 (an ~8.5x gap), 81% of rows have |within| < 0.10, and sign(rho_full) equals sign(between) in 245/245 rows. Coarse-support covariates give the cleanest cases -- pH ~ climate_MAP has within = 0.06 yet between = 0.682 and is year-stable (0.649/0.605); a 9-km covariate is near-constant within a field and cannot carry sub-field information by construction. The same band families that generalize for one target are zonal_only for another (pH ~ s2_NDRE_spring: within = -0.033, between = -0.709), so the distinction is target-by-feature regional structure, not the band itself. These features track the macro-climatic gradient driving chernozem-to-kastanozem zonation; they inflate any spatially pooled metric while contributing nothing under farm-mean centring -- the pseudoreplication that block and farm-level validation expose (Roberts et al. 2017; Meyer et al. 2018, 2019; Wadoux et al. 2021).

**Weak (76%): honest negatives plus demoted confounds.** Most weak pairs are genuinely flat -- sensor-physics mismatches where the band, texture, or season carries no channel coupled to the property. A minority are real regional gradients demoted for failing the year gate: the tiny clustered 2022 sample inflates correlations (median |rho_2022| = 0.236 vs |rho_2023| = 0.157; 1162 rows reach |rho| >= 0.30 in 2022 versus 572 in 2023, twice as many on one-fifth the samples), so a strong 2022 arm collapses or reverses in 2023 and the pooled correlation nulls out.

**Implications.** Transferable local skill is the thin generalizable tail (31 robust pairs), confined to spring bare-soil chromophores for the stable, mineralogically anchored properties and absent for sulfur entirely; everything else is regional gradient or design artefact that random or field-level cross-validation would silently reward (Meyer et al. 2019; Ploton et al. 2020). This grounds the paper's thesis on the intrinsic limits of optical remote sensing for digital soil mapping: what is mappable is set by chromophore physics and sampling design, not model capacity. In practice, screened correlations should be reported with their between/within decomposition and validated under farm-LOFO before being claimed predictive; mobile, non-chromophoric analytes (NO3, S) should be treated as canopy- or management-mediated rather than directly sensed; and feature selection should prioritise season-matched bare-soil spectra over regionally smoothed or temporally aggregated covariates.
