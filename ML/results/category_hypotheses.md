# Feature-category hypotheses (Workflow A: 4 categories x 2 lenses, 44 hypotheses)


## generalizable  —  lens: PEDOLOGICAL / RS-PHYSICS / AGRONOMIC
**Key takeaway:** The "generalizable" set is dominated by SPRING bare-soil and growing-season spectral features, and the WHY differs sharply by property lability. For STABLE properties (pH, K2O, P2O5, SOC), the local signal is genuine soil-physics: spring SWIR/NIR/red-edge bands and BSI/MSI directly sense the soil surface (carbonates, clay, Fe-oxides, residue darkness) when canopy is minimal, so |block_within| survives spatial CV (e.g. l8_GNDVI_spring pH within=-0.323; s2_BSI_spring K2O within=-0.271; s2_B12_spring K2O within=-0.263). For LABILE NO3, almost no feature senses nitrate directly; instead the robust spring-band correlations are crop/management proxies (residual fertility, early biomass) and the strongest tell is that growing-season GNDVI/NDVI means show within-field signal of OPPOSITE sign to the regional gradient (ts_s2_GNDVI_mean NO3 within=+0.283 vs between=-0.452) — i.e. within a field, greener = more N, while across the region the zonal climate gradient dominates and reverses. S has ZERO generalizable features, exactly as RS physics predicts (sulfate has no diagnostic 400-2500 nm absorption). GLCM texture features generalize for pH/P2O5/K2O because tillage/residue micro-patterning is management-stable across years. Hypotheses are property-specific and mostly medium-to-low confidence pending the within-block-sign vs pooled-sign check and a residual-fertility ground-truth test.

- **[H1] (medium)** Stable properties (pH, K2O, P2O5, SOC) carry their generalizable local signal almost exclusively in SPRING bare-soil/low-canopy spectral features: SWIR, NIR, red-edge bands and soil indices. Examples (block_within survives spatial CV): l8_GNDVI_spring pH (full=-0.67, within=-0.323, betw=-0.703, robust); l8_SAVI_spring pH (full=-0.482, within=-0.302); s2_BSI_spring K2O (full=-0.478, within=-0.271, robust); s2_B12_spring K2O (within=-0.263); s2_B11_spring K2O (within=-0.184); s2_MSI_spring K2O (within=-0.246). Spring dominates the category (34 of 96) and the robust subset is 18/20 spring spectral bands.
    - *Mechanism:* In spring (Mar-May), the steppe surface is largely bare or sparsely vegetated, so the sensor sees SOIL not canopy. Soil chromophores then drive reflectance: carbonate and clay mineral overtones in SWIR (B11~1.6um, B12~2.2um), Fe-oxide and organic-matter darkening in VIS-NIR. These are physically tied to the SAME pedogenic factors that set pH (carbonate content), exchangeable K (clay/illite mineralogy and CEC) and P sorption (Fe/Al oxides, carbonate). Because the soil mineralogy/OM does not change between 2022 and 2023, the spring spectral-to-stable-property mapping is year-stable and partly LOCAL (within-field brightness contrasts track within-field texture/OM contrasts), not purely a regional gradient. GNDVI/SAVI in near-bare spring act as inverse soil-brightness indices here rather than biomass indices.
    - *Evidence:* Spring spectral bands give the only robust within-CV signals for stable targets: K2O robust set = s2_BSI/B11/B12/MSI/NBR/NDMI_spring (within 0.24-0.29). pH l8_GNDVI_spring within=-0.323, year-consistent (22=-0.294, 23=-0.615). SWIR clay index s2_MSI_spring and SWIR ratio spectral_B11_B8_spring both within~-0.24. Compare zonal_only pH features (s2_NDRE_spring full=-0.52 but within=-0.033) which are pure regional gradients — the difference is precisely that the generalizable spring bands retain LOCAL contrast.
    - *Test:* On spectral library / lab data: spring SWIR reflectance (B11,B12) should correlate with measured CaCO3 and clay% within individual fields after removing field means; if true, partialling out CaCO3/clay should collapse the pH/K2O within-block correlation toward zero. Also predict autumn bare-soil bands should give a WEAKER but same-sign within signal (fewer autumn features qualified: 3 vs 34 spring), testable by re-running the within-block Spearman on autumn-only acquisitions.
- **[H2] (medium)** For LABILE NO3, growing-season VEGETATION-INDEX MEANS (GNDVI/NDVI/SAVI temporal means) show a within-field correlation of OPPOSITE SIGN to their regional (between-block) correlation: ts_s2_GNDVI_mean (within=+0.283, betw=-0.452, full=-0.236); ts_l8_GNDVI_mean (within=+0.292, betw=-0.459); ts_s2_NDVI_mean / ts_s2_SAVI_mean (within=+0.248, betw=-0.288); ts_l8_NDVI_mean (within=+0.282, betw=-0.362). The pooled rho is small/negative because the two effects partly cancel.
    - *Mechanism:* Two physically distinct N signals are superimposed. (1) REGIONAL/zonal: across the climate gradient, the more productive (greener, higher-rainfall) zones have N more fully taken up and leached, so high regional NDVI co-occurs with lower residual soil NO3 -> negative between-block. (2) LOCAL/agronomic: within a single field, patches with more vigorous crop/biomass are the better-fertilized, higher-mineralization patches, so greener pixels overlie higher residual NO3 -> positive within-block. NO3 is mobile and tracks the crop's own N status, which is exactly what a vegetation index proxies at the within-field scale. This sign reversal is the fingerprint of a labile nutrient sensed indirectly through the crop rather than through the soil.
    - *Evidence:* All five GNDVI/NDVI/SAVI temporal-mean rows for NO3 show within~+0.25 to +0.29 against between~-0.29 to -0.46, year-consistent in sign of the (small negative) pooled rho. By contrast the same GNDVI_spring features are zonal_only for pH (within~0) — i.e. the within>0 effect is NO3-specific, consistent with a crop-N rather than soil-mineral mechanism. ts_s2_NDWI_mean for NO3 mirrors this with the opposite polarity (within=-0.243, betw=+0.414), consistent with canopy-water acting as the same biomass proxy with flipped sign.
    - *Test:* Compute within-field Spearman(NDVI, NO3) field-by-field; predict the median within-field coefficient is POSITIVE while the field-mean NDVI vs field-mean NO3 coefficient is NEGATIVE. Additionally, NO3 should be the only target showing this systematic within/between sign reversal for VI means (pH/SOC/K2O should not). If NO3 within-block sign also matches pooled sign it would FAIL the robust criterion — indeed none of these VI-mean NO3 features are flagged robust, supporting that they are real-but-fragile crop proxies.
- **[H3] (high)** Sulfur (S) has ZERO generalizable features (per target counts: NO3 44, K2O 27, pH 13, P2O5 8, SOC 4, S 0), while S appears 37 times in zonal_only and 77 in unstable.
    - *Mechanism:* Sulfate (the plant-available, lab-measured S pool) has NO diagnostic electronic or vibrational absorption in the 400-2500 nm range that Sentinel-2/Landsat-8 sample, so there is no direct soil-chromophore pathway to a LOCAL S signal. Whatever S correlations exist must run through proxies: regional climate/atmospheric-deposition gradients (-> zonal_only) or year-specific fertilization history and weather-driven mineralization (-> unstable). Because S is labile and management/deposition-driven, and because year and location are confounded in this design, no S feature can simultaneously have local within-block signal AND year-consistency.
    - *Evidence:* S is entirely absent from generalizable and from robust generalizable, the only one of six properties with this property. Its zonal_only features are all canopy/biomass VIs (l8_SAVI_late_summer S full=0.383 within=0.077 betw=0.653; ts_l8_NDVI_mean S betw=0.651) — high between, near-zero within, i.e. pure regional gradient with no local sensing. Its unstable features (l8_SR_B4_spring S 22=+0.317, 23=-0.36) flip sign across years.
    - *Test:* S should remain unmappable at the local scale under any spectral feature set: predict that adding hyperspectral/SWIR-derived features will NOT create a stable within-block S signal, whereas it would for clay/carbonate-linked pH and K2O. A successful local S model, if ever achieved, must derive predictive power from non-spectral covariates (fertilization records, deposition maps), not from reflectance — testable by feature-ablation showing S R2 is unchanged when spectral bands are removed.
- **[H4] (low)** GLCM TEXTURE features generalize (carry within-block signal) for stable properties pH, P2O5, K2O and partly NO3: glcm_glcm_nir_idm_late_summer pH (within=-0.186, robust); glcm_glcm_red_asm_spring pH (within=-0.168, robust); glcm_glcm_nir_ent_summer P2O5 (within=-0.199, robust); glcm_glcm_nir_asm_summer P2O5 (within=+0.198, robust); glcm_glcm_nir_idm_summer P2O5 (within=+0.162, robust); plus several K2O texture rows (within 0.16-0.26). Texture is 20 of 96 generalizable and 8 of the robust subset are texture/spectral.
    - *Mechanism:* GLCM homogeneity/ASM/entropy quantify the spatial micro-pattern of the surface (residue clumping, tillage furrow regularity, crusting, vegetation patchiness). These patterns are set by stable, management-and-pedology-linked factors: clay/carbonate soils crust and aggregate differently (affecting P sorption and K-bearing clay), and a given field's tillage/residue regime is consistent year to year. Texture is therefore an indirect but year-stable structural proxy for soil physical condition that is genuinely LOCAL (it is computed from within-field pixel neighborhoods), so it survives block-mean removal where a single-band brightness might not.
    - *Evidence:* Texture features are over-represented in robust generalizable (P2O5's only robust signals are 3 GLCM-NIR features; pH gains glcm_glcm_nir_idm_late_summer and glcm_glcm_red_asm_spring as robust). Homogeneity (IDM/ASM) and entropy give paired opposite-sign within coefficients (P2O5: nir_ent within=-0.199 vs nir_asm within=+0.198) as expected since ASM and entropy are mathematical inverses — internal consistency that argues against noise.
    - *Test:* Texture's contribution should be mechanistically redundant with surface-condition variables: predict that within-field GLCM homogeneity correlates with measured aggregate stability / crusting and with residue cover, and that conditioning on residue cover (e.g. a tillage/cellulose absorption index) reduces the texture-to-P2O5/K2O within-block correlation. If texture were pure noise it would not be year-consistent — so the falsifier is finding that its 2022 and 2023 signs disagree on a held-out farm set.
- **[H5] (medium)** Some CLIMATIC and TOPOGRAPHIC features were tagged generalizable but show within-block signal OPPOSITE in sign to (and decoupled from) their regional gradient, and are NOT robust: climate_MAT P2O5 (full=+0.33, betw=+0.577, within=-0.202); climate_MAP K2O (full=+0.208, betw=+0.345, within=-0.267); climate_MAP P2O5 (within=-0.182); topo_aspect_sin SOC (full=0.1, within=-0.162, 22=+0.635, 23=+0.106); topo_DEM pH (full=0.382, betw=0.563, within=-0.195). ERA5 is ~9 km and SoilGrids 250 m.
    - *Mechanism:* These coarse covariates physically encode REGIONAL gradients only (their between-block rho is large), so a real local mechanism is implausible at 9 km / 250 m resolution finer than a field. The small negative within-block coefficient is most likely a statistical artifact of removing 30 k-means block means from a near-constant-within-block covariate: after de-meaning, the residual is dominated by block-assignment noise and the tiny remaining variance can correlate weakly and incidentally with the target. The year-consistency that admitted them to 'generalizable' is satisfied trivially because a static climate/elevation layer has identical values in both years, so sign cannot flip. These are FALSE generalizables — zonal features mis-tagged because the year-consistency gate cannot discriminate truly-local from spatially-static covariates.
    - *Evidence:* MAT/MAP/DEM all have between-block rho 2-3x their |within|, the signature of regional gradients; their within signs (negative) contradict their between signs (positive), which no coherent local soil mechanism predicts. topo_aspect_sin SOC has 22=+0.635 vs 23=+0.106 — an order-of-magnitude amplitude collapse that betrays year/location confounding rather than a stable local effect. None are in the robust subset (which additionally requires |rho_full|>=0.30 and within-sign==pooled-sign), and indeed climate_MAP K2O within(-0.267) has OPPOSITE sign to pooled(+0.208).
    - *Test:* Predict these features fail any stricter local-validity test: under farm-LOFO their predictive contribution should vanish or invert, and a permutation test on within-block residuals should not exceed chance. Concretely, the within-block sign should NOT equal the pooled sign for MAT/MAP/DEM here (already true), so they should be reclassified out of generalizable if the robust 'within-sign==pooled-sign' rule were applied to the base category. Re-tag using a within-block effective-sample check (variance of the covariate within blocks) to filter spatially-static layers.

## generalizable  —  lens: STATISTICAL / SAMPLING-DESIGN / SPATIAL: reasoning from the 2022x2023 year-location confound (5 vs 15 disjoint farms), spatial autocorrelation & pseudoreplication, support/scale mismatch (ERA5 9 km, SoilGrids 250 m), Simpson-paradox within/between sign reversals, winner's-curse multiplicity over 512 features, and why between-block survives while within-block collapses.
**Key takeaway:** Through the statistical/sampling-design lens, "generalizable" splits sharply in two. The genuine core is the robust subset (n=31), dominated by SPRING spectral features (26/31 spring; 17/31 Spectral-base) where block_within and block_between share a sign and |within|>=0.17 — these transfer because ~75% of samples were taken in spring, so a spring bare-soil image observes the field in the same state as the lab sample (season-matched support). This works for stable pH/K2O genuinely, and for labile NO3 ONLY via spring NIR/red-edge bands. The remaining ~65 rows are largely artifacts: (a) NO3 temporal-mean features (ts_*_GNDVI/NDVI_mean) with within/between SIGN FLIPS and near-zero per-year rho (Simpson/ecological-fallacy residual noise); (b) coarse-support covariates (ERA5 9 km MAT/MAP, SRTM DEM) whose real signal is purely zonal — their small opposite-sign within-block value is a detrending artifact; (c) 2022-inflated estimates (|rho_2022|>|rho_2023| in 67/96, median ratio 1.3) from only 5 farms / 174 samples, where spatial pseudoreplication + winner's curse over 512 features make "year-consistency" cheap. Sulfur (S) has zero generalizable features, consistent with its lack of diagnostic spectral absorption. Recommendation: trust the robust spring-spectral subset; re-test everything else with season-matched subsampling, farm-cluster bootstrap CIs, and K-robustness of the block partition before claiming transferable local signal.

- **[H1] (high)** The TRULY generalizable core is the robust subset (n=31), which is 26/31 spring and 17/31 Spectral-base, NOT the full n=96. The robust NO3 features are spring NIR/red-edge bands (s2_B6/B7/B8/B8A_spring, s2_EVI_spring within=-0.207, s2_SAVI_spring within=-0.207, l8_SR_B5_spring within=-0.259) and robust K2O are spring shortwave/bare-soil indices (s2_BSI_spring within=-0.271, s2_B12_spring within=-0.263, s2_NDMI_spring within=+0.246). These are the only cases where block_within and block_between carry the SAME sign and |within|>=0.17.
    - *Mechanism:* ~75% of samples (817/1085) were collected in spring (Mar-May), so a SPRING image of bare/early-vegetation soil observes the field in nearly the same state the lab sample was taken. Acquisition-season aligned with sampling-season minimizes the temporal mismatch that destroys correlations for other seasons. Spring bare-soil also exposes the surface directly (BSI/SWIR sees mineralogy/moisture; NIR/red-edge sees early biomass that integrates N supply), giving a genuine pixel-local soil signal that survives spatial-block detrending.
    - *Evidence:* Robust season counts spring=26 vs summer=3, late-summer=1; robust group Spectral-base=17, Spectral-composite=8, Temporal=1. Robust NO3 are all spring bands with within in [-0.17,-0.26] and SAME-sign between. Overall generalizable median|within| is only 0.18-0.20 and is EXCEEDED by median|between| for every property (pH 0.19 vs 0.42; NO3 0.18 vs 0.28), so the n=96 set as a whole is between-dominated; the spring spectral robust core is the exception.
    - *Test:* Recompute block_within separately on the spring-only subsample (n~817) vs autumn-only (n~268). The robust spring spectral features should retain |within|>=0.15 in the spring subsample but collapse toward 0 when correlated against autumn-collected samples; non-spring 'generalizable' features should not survive this season-matched test.
- **[H2] (high)** A large block of NO3 'generalizable' rows are season-aggregated vegetation-index MEANS (ts_l8_GNDVI_mean within=+0.292 betw=-0.459; ts_s2_GNDVI_mean within=+0.283 betw=-0.452; ts_l8/s2_NDVI_mean, ts_*_SAVI_mean within~+0.25) where block_within and block_between have OPPOSITE signs and both per-year rho are tiny (|rho_2022|,|rho_2023| < 0.20). These are Simpson/ecological-fallacy reversals, not transferable local signal.
    - *Mechanism:* Between blocks, regional productivity gradients give negative GNDVI~NO3 (greener zones = depleted/leached nitrate at the zonal scale). After removing block means, the surviving positive within-block correlation is a residual-rank artifact: with 30 k-means blocks absorbing the dominant variance, the leftover within-block ranks are dominated by noise and a weak opposite-sign micro-pattern. The category rule (|within|>=0.15 AND per-year signs agree) admits these because the per-year SIGNS happen to agree even though the per-year MAGNITUDES are ~0.1-0.15 (statistically near-zero).
    - *Evidence:* 26/96 generalizable rows have within/full sign FLIP; the NO3 ts_*_mean cluster is the bulk of them. ts_l8_GNDVI_mean NO3: full=-0.224, within=+0.292, betw=-0.459, 22=-0.135, 23=-0.105 -> within sign opposes BOTH per-year correlations. 15 generalizable rows have BOTH |rho_year|<0.20, and the ts_*_mean / s2_B3_summer NO3 entries dominate that list.
    - *Test:* Bootstrap block_within for the ts_*_GNDVI_mean NO3 features by resampling fields; the 95% CI should include 0 (or straddle the per-year-consistent sign), and re-running k-means with a different seed / different K (e.g. 10, 50 blocks) should change the within-block sign. Robust spring spectral features should be stable to these perturbations; the ts_mean cluster should not.
- **[H3] (high)** Coarse-support covariates leak into 'generalizable' as pure between-block artifacts with within/between sign flips: climate_MAT P2O5 (within=-0.202, betw=+0.577, full=+0.330), climate_MAP K2O (within=-0.267, betw=+0.345), topo_DEM pH (within=-0.195, betw=+0.563, 22=+0.723). For these the within-block magnitude is small and OPPOSITE to the dominant regional effect.
    - *Mechanism:* ERA5-Land (~9 km) and SRTM/DEM vary almost only between farms, not within a 30-block neighborhood, so their genuine information is entirely the zonal/regional component (large positive block_between). The negative block_within is a spurious detrending residual: removing block means from a covariate that is nearly constant within a block leaves only sampling noise, which can correlate at |0.15-0.27| by chance over 512 features (winner's curse). The category gate misclassifies them as 'generalizable' because year-consistency is satisfied by the strong between-driven per-year correlations.
    - *Evidence:* climate_MAT P2O5: between (0.577) is ~3x within (0.202) and opposite sign; topo_DEM pH 22=+0.723 vs 23=+0.321 shows the per-year strength is carried by the 5-farm 2022 zonal contrast. All three coarse covariates appear with |between| >> |within| and within-sign flipped — the signature of support/scale mismatch, not a local mechanism. A 9 km or 250 m covariate cannot physically carry a field-internal gradient.
    - *Test:* Replace the k-means block means with a variogram-range-matched spatial filter (remove variation at scales > ERA5/DEM support). The within-block correlation of climate_MAT/MAP and topo_DEM should drop to ~0 (no residual local signal), confirming the within value is a detrending artifact rather than a transferable local association.
- **[H4] (medium)** 2022 per-year correlations are systematically inflated relative to 2023 across the generalizable set: |rho_2022| > |rho_2023| in 67/96 rows, median |rho_2022|/|rho_2023| = 1.3. Extreme cases: topo_aspect_sin SOC (22=+0.635 vs 23=+0.106), topo_DEM pH (22=+0.723 vs 23=+0.321), s2_EVI_spring NO3 (22=-0.577 vs 23=-0.264), glcm_glcm_nir_idm_late_summer pH (22=-0.733 vs 23=-0.300).
    - *Mechanism:* 2022 has only 174 samples across 5 farms / 18 fields. With so few effectively-independent spatial units (n_eff ~ number of farms, not samples, due to spatial autocorrelation/pseudoreplication), Spearman estimates are high-variance and the largest of 512 screened features are upward-biased (winner's curse). Year-consistency is then easy to satisfy because the noisy-but-large 2022 estimate agrees in sign with the more stable 15-farm 2023 estimate, even when 2023 magnitude is modest. This makes the 'year-stable' label partly a small-sample / multiplicity artifact rather than evidence of mechanism.
    - *Evidence:* 67/96 rows 2022-stronger; median ratio 1.3. The SOC subset (only n=4 generalizable) has median|rho_2022|=0.545 vs |rho_2023|=0.208 — a >2.6x inflation on the year confounded with just 5 farms. topo_aspect_sin SOC within=-0.162 but 22=+0.635 (opposite sign to within) shows the 2022 number is a 5-farm zonal coincidence, not a local effect.
    - *Test:* For each generalizable feature, recompute per-year Spearman with farm-level cluster bootstrap (resample farms, not samples) and report effective-df-corrected CIs. The 2022 CIs should be much wider; many 'year-consistent' labels should become indeterminate (CI crosses 0 in at least one year), preferentially for SOC/pH topographic and late-summer texture features. Robust spring NO3/K2O spectral features should keep both-year CIs away from 0.
- **[H5] (high)** Property-specific split in WHY features generalize. STABLE properties (pH, K2O) generalize via genuine surface-spectral mechanisms in spring (robust pH: l8_GNDVI/NDVI/SAVI_spring within -0.19..-0.32; robust K2O: spring BSI/SWIR within -0.18..-0.29). NO3 is mobile (weeks-scale), so only a spring image taken near the spring sampling window captures a coherent nitrate-vegetation state; aggregated/temporal NO3 features have no stable physical referent and surface only as detrending noise. S has no diagnostic absorption 400-2500 nm and is driven by fertilization history, so no spectral feature can carry a transferable local S signal — hence its total absence from the generalizable set.
    - *Evidence:* Generalizable per-target counts: NO3=44, K2O=27, pH=13, P2O5=8, SOC=4, S=0. S is entirely absent here (it has 37 zonal_only and 77 unstable rows). NO3 generalizable group mix is Temporal=19 (the H2 artifact cluster) vs Spectral-base=13 (the robust spring core); robust NO3 are exclusively spring spectral bands. Robust subset has K2O=10, NO3=12, pH=6, P2O5=3 and NO SOC, NO S.

- **[H6] (high)** Within-block collapses but between-block survives across the WHOLE feature set, and the generalizable category is essentially the thin tail where some within-block signal persists. For generalizable, |between| exceeds |within| in 72/96 rows; median|between| > median|within| for every property (pH 0.42 vs 0.19; SOC 0.39 vs 0.16; P2O5 0.37 vs 0.19).

## zonal_only  —  lens: PEDOLOGICAL / RS-PHYSICS / AGRONOMIC
**Key takeaway:** zonal_only features are not soil sensors but geography sensors: they encode the macro-climatic and relief gradient that drives the chernozem-to-kastanozem zonation (MAP, slope, GS_temp, aspect/curvature) and its biomass/management shadow (spring red-edge/greenness for pH, K2O, NO3; greenness/NDWI for S). The defining number is the order-of-magnitude collapse from mean |block_between| ~0.47-0.55 to mean |block_within| ~0.02-0.08 for every target, with 208/245 rows sign-stable across years -- so the regional signal is real but carries zero local, transferable information. Mechanistically this is expected: pH/SOC/K2O/P2O5 are stable, zonally distributed soil-forming outcomes that coarse covariates capture only as a smooth gradient, while S has no spectral chromophore at all and can only be reached through a confounded deposition/intensity proxy. The honest conclusion is that these features will inflate any spatially-pooled metric and must be dropped (or partialled against MAP/elevation/slope) before farm-LOFO evaluation.

- **[Z1] (high)** The class-defining signature across all six targets: mean |block_between| ~0.47-0.55 but mean |block_within| ~0.02-0.08 (K2O 0.548 vs 0.037; pH 0.487 vs 0.062; S 0.494 vs 0.069; SOC 0.506 vs 0.079; NO3 0.470 vs 0.024; P2O5 0.466 vs 0.050). Pure soil-forming covariates carry it: climate_MAP-pH full=0.659/betw=0.682/within=0.060; topo_slope-pH full=0.609/betw=0.524/within=-0.048; climate_GS_temp-P2O5 betw=0.639/within=-0.021; topo_profile_curvature-SOC betw=0.55/within=-0.042. Stable properties only (pH, SOC, K2O, P2O5).
    - *Mechanism:* Steppe soil chemistry follows the classic zonal chernozem->kastanozem sequence set by the macro-climatic moisture/temperature gradient and regional relief. MAP, growing-season temperature and landscape position govern leaching depth, carbonate accumulation and humus content over kilometres. These covariates are smooth at the regional scale and near-constant inside one k-means block (~one locality), so they carry the between-block (zonal) variance but have no within-block gradient to exploit. This is geography, not a sensed soil property.

**Key takeaway:** The "unstable" category is, mechanistically, the bucket where the confounded YEAR x LOCATION design plus 2022's tiny geographically-clustered n (174 samples / 5 farms vs 911 / 15 farms) manufactures sign reversals: the pooled correlation matches the n-dominant 2023 sign in 387/404 rows, sign counts mirror exactly between years, and 64% of rows have |block_within|<0.10 - i.e. almost all the "signal" is regional/zonal, not local. For sulfate the correlation should collapse to ~0 in both years regardless of masking (no chromophore); for pH/carbonate-linked colour, a within-farm correlation may survive only if the carbonate/Fe-oxide chromophore (e.g. ~2330 nm carbonate, redness index) is the true driver - test by substituting a physically-grounded redness/carbonate index and checking sign stability.
- **[H4] (medium)** Cross-season DELTA and temporal-range/std features that are the most flip-prone class. 72 of 404 unstable rows are delta_ features and 116 carry cross_season_concern=True. Examples: delta_spectral_NDVI_spring_to_autumn~pH (within=-0.075, 22=+0.631, 23=-0.021); delta_s2_BSI_spring_to_summer~K2O (within=0.146, betw=0.317, 22=-0.378, 23=0.418); range_s2_BSI~K2O (within=-0.168, 22=0.38, 23=-0.346); ts_s2_BSI_std~K2O (within=-0.155, 22=0.447, 23=-0.342); ts_s2_MSI_std/cv~NO3 (22=+0.55/+0.49, 23=-0.19/-0.20).
    - *Mechanism:* Delta and temporal-variability features encode MANAGEMENT and WEATHER dynamics (green-up timing, tillage events, crop rotation, fertilisation, growing-season rainfall variability), not a static soil property. Their magnitude depends on the crop calendar and the specific weather of each season. Because 2022 and 2023 are different years on different farms (different rotations, different rainfall, possibly different image-pair dates), the spring-to-summer/autumn trajectory has a different meaning each year, so a delta that ranks fields one way in 2022 ranks them oppositely in 2023. For STABLE soil properties (pH, K2O) the soil did not change - so any delta-property correlation is necessarily an incidental management/weather confound and is expected to be non-transferable.
    - *Evidence:* Delta/range/ts-std features are over-represented among unstable (Temporal group = 156/404, the single largest, plus 84 Spectral-composite) and their 2022 magnitudes are often large (0.38-0.63) while within-block signal is small-to-moderate (0.07-0.17), so the flip lives in the between/regional component driven by year-specific management+weather. The BSI delta/range/std cluster for K2O flips coherently (22 positive ~ +0.38-0.45, 23 negative ~ -0.34), indicating a shared management-dynamics driver rather than independent noise.
    - *Test:* Join field-level agronomic records (crop type, tillage date, fertiliser application, sowing date) and growing-season rainfall for each year. Conditioning the spring-to-summer NDVI/BSI delta on crop+rainfall should remove the year contrast; residual delta-property correlation for stable pH/K2O should drop to ~0. If records are unavailable, restrict to fields with the same crop in both years - the delta sign should then agree across years.
- **[H5] (high)** The whole unstable category is structurally a SAMPLE-IMBALANCE x SPATIAL-CONFOUNDING artifact rather than a per-feature physical phenomenon. Mirror-symmetric sign counts (2022: 229 pos / 175 neg; 2023: 175 pos / 229 neg), full sign matches 2023 in 387/404 and 2022 in only 17/404, and 259/404 rows have |within|<0.10.
    - *Mechanism:* 2022 (174 samples, 5 farms) and 2023 (911 samples, 15 farms) are spatially disjoint, so the pooled correlation is overwhelmingly the 2023 between-farm gradient. A feature lands in 'unstable' whenever its weak/regional association happens to have opposite sign in the small 2022 farm cluster - which, for a low-|rho|, near-zero-within feature, is essentially a coin-flip set by which corner of the zonal gradient the 5 small-n farms occupy. Thus 'unstable' is less a physical category than the bucket where year x location confounding plus 2022's tiny, geographically clustered n produces an apparent reversal. This applies most to stable properties (pH/S/K2O/P2O5/SOC: mean |within| 0.04-0.09) and least to NO3 (0.16, where a real scale-split mechanism, H2, also contributes).
    - *Evidence:* mean |within| by target among unstable: S=0.040, SOC=0.047, pH=0.082, K2O=0.089, P2O5=0.086, NO3=0.160. The 0.96 agreement of full-sign with the n-dominant year, the exact pos/neg mirror between years, and 64% of rows having |within|<0.10 are all hallmarks of an n-weighted regional confound, not feature-specific physics. 368/404 are leakage_suspect=YES.
    - *Test:* Bootstrap-subsample 2023 down to 174 samples drawn from 5 farms and recompute per-year correlations: many 'unstable' flips should appear or disappear depending on which farms are drawn, demonstrating the sign is sampling-determined. Conversely, under a proper farm-blocked CV (farm-LOFO), these features should show near-zero, sign-unstable importance - confirming they carry no transferable signal. A feature with a true mechanism (e.g. NO3 summer-VI within-signal, H2) should retain a stable within-farm sign under this resampling while the pure-confound features (H1, H3) do not.

## unstable  —  lens: STATISTICAL / SAMPLING-DESIGN / SPATIAL — reasoning from the YEAR×LOCATION confound (2022 = 5 farms/18 fields/174 samples vs 2023 = 15 disjoint farms/64 fields/911 samples), spatial pseudoreplication, support/scale mismatch, Simpson-paradox sign reversals, and winner's-curse over 512 features. Distinguishing artefactual sign-flips from genuine year-to-year non-stationarity.
**Key takeaway:** The 'unstable' (sign-flipping) category is overwhelmingly a SAMPLING-DESIGN artefact, not evidence of real biophysical year-to-year change. The smoking gun: physically time-invariant covariates (topo_aspect_sin, topo_slope, climate_MAP) flip sign against slow-changing properties (pH +0.825→-0.512; SOC +0.725→-0.341) — impossible unless the flip comes from the YEAR×LOCATION confound, because 2022 (5 farms) and 2023 (15 farms) are spatially disjoint and sit on different limbs of the regional gradient (Simpson's paradox). Statistically, these features carry almost no local signal (median |block_within|=0.070, 77% below 0.15) and live almost entirely between farms (median |block_between|=0.370, 4.7x within) — the same zonal-only signature, but the regional estimate is unreplicated: with only ~5 effectively-independent farms in 2022 (critical |rho|≈0.15 uncorrected, 0.27 Bonferroni over 512 features) the 2022 correlation is noise-dominated and winner's-cursed, so it regresses past zero on the cleaner 911-sample 2023 leg. A genuine, non-artefactual component exists ONLY for labile targets (NO3, S = 200/404 rows: mobile nitrate, sulfate with no diagnostic absorption) where temporal lability and acquisition/phenology shifts add real non-stationarity — but even there it is perfectly aliased with the spatial confound and cannot be separated without matched-field or within-year tests. Bottom line: do not trust per-year sign as a transferability signal here; the design cannot distinguish 'changed in time' from 'different place sampled', and these features should be excluded from any model expected to generalize to new farms/years.

- **[H1] (high)** The defining property of EVERY unstable feature is a 2022 vs 2023 sign flip, and the cleanest cases are PHYSICALLY TIME-INVARIANT covariates correlating with STABLE soil properties — topo_aspect_sin/pH (22=+0.825, 23=-0.512), climate_MAP/SOC (22=+0.725, 23=-0.341), topo_slope/SOC (22=+0.663, 23=-0.481), topo_aspect_cos/SOC (22=-0.642, 23=+0.620), climate_GS_precip/P2O5 (22=-0.579, 23=+0.468). Slope, aspect and long-term MAP at a field do NOT change between years, and pH/SOC barely move over one season, so the FEATURE value and the TARGET value at each field are essentially identical across years.
    - *Mechanism:* Pure YEAR×LOCATION confound. Because 2022 (5 farms/18 fields) and 2023 (15 farms/64 fields) are spatially DISJOINT (~1 shared field), rho_2022 and rho_2023 are computed over completely different field sets sitting in different parts of the regional gradient. When neither covariate nor property changes in time, a sign reversal cannot be a temporal/biophysical effect — it can ONLY arise because the two farm clusters occupy opposite limbs of a non-linear or multi-cluster regional relationship. This is a Simpson's-paradox reversal driven by which 5 vs which 15 farms were sampled, not by anything happening to the soil.
    - *Evidence:* 10 static (Topographic+Climatic) features are tagged unstable; topo_aspect_sin/pH flips +0.825→-0.512 despite aspect being a fixed terrain attribute and pH-KCl being a slow-changing property. climate_MAP/SOC flips +0.725→-0.341 though MAP is a multi-decadal climatology and SOC turns over on decadal scales. Median |block_between|=0.370 vs median |block_within|=0.070 (4.7x) confirms the surviving signal is regional/between-farm, exactly the axis the disjoint-year design scrambles.
    - *Test:* Restrict to the ~1 field (or any fields) sampled in BOTH years and recompute rho_2022 and rho_2023 on that matched subset: for static covariates the per-year correlations should converge (no flip). Equivalently, regress the property on farm-mean covariate and confirm 2022 farms and 2023 farms fall on opposite sides of a curved/multi-modal regional trend. If the flip persists on matched fields, H1 is refuted.
- **[H2] (high)** Unstable features have median |block_within|=0.070 (77% have |within|<0.15) but median |block_between|=0.370 — a between/within ratio of 4.7, essentially identical to the zonal_only category (within 0.050, between 0.482). The ONLY thing separating 'unstable' from 'zonal_only' is that the regional correlation reverses sign across years rather than holding.
    - *Mechanism:* Spatial pseudoreplication + support/scale mismatch. With only 30 k-means blocks and farms being the real spatial unit, almost all signal lives between blocks/farms (the regional gradient), not within. Coarse covariates — ERA5 climate at ~9 km and SoilGrids at 250 m — are spatially smoothed to near-constant within a farm, so they carry essentially zero local information and act purely as regional/zonal proxies. The effective sample size is the number of independent farms (5 in 2022, 15 in 2023), NOT 174/911 samples. A between-farm correlation estimated from only ~5 effectively-independent points in 2022 is wildly unstable and easily flips sign relative to the 15-farm 2023 estimate.
    - *Evidence:* climate_GS_precip/P2O5 has block_within=-0.265 but block_between=+0.669 with a full-sign flip (22=-0.579, 23=+0.468) — a coarse 9 km covariate that is a regional gradient only. ts_s2_IRECI_cv/NO3 keeps a small honest within (0.212) but its between (-0.405) and year values (22=+0.369, 23=-0.272) flip. The cross-category table shows unstable and zonal_only share the same within-collapse signature; the flip is the regional estimate being unreplicated, not local signal turning over.
    - *Test:* Compute the effective number of independent spatial units per year (e.g., via variogram range or simply farm count) and the sampling SE of a between-farm Spearman rho. Predicted: 95% CIs on rho_2022 for unstable features are so wide (driven by n_farm≈5) that they overlap zero and overlap rho_2023. Re-estimating correlations at the FARM-MEAN level (one point per farm) with cluster-robust CIs should show the 2022/2023 estimates are statistically indistinguishable — i.e., the 'flip' is noise, not a real difference.
- **[H3] (medium)** 404 features are tagged unstable out of 512 screened against 6 targets (3072 rows). The 2022 leg has only 174 samples across 5 farms, and unstable features show 9 cases with |rho_2022|>=0.6 and 20 with |rho_2022|>=0.5 — large apparent correlations on the small-n year.
    - *Mechanism:* Winner's-curse / multiple-comparisons inflation on the small 2022 sample. The critical |rho| for nominal p=0.05 at n=174 is ~0.149 and the Bonferroni (512 features) threshold is ~0.265; at n=911 these drop to ~0.065 and ~0.115. Screening 512 features per target means dozens will cross any fixed threshold by chance in the noisier 2022 leg, and the ones that do are upward-biased in magnitude (selected on the noise). When the SAME feature is evaluated on the cleaner 2023 leg, regression-to-the-mean pulls it back — and because the 2022 estimate was largely noise around ~0, it lands on the other side, producing a sign flip. This is amplified by the n imbalance (174 vs 911): the pooled rho_full is dominated by 2023, so 2022 often disagrees with both.
    - *Evidence:* Median |rho_2022|=0.203 sits right at the n=174 noise floor (~0.149 uncorrected); 20/404 unstable features exceed 0.5 in 2022 vs 16 in 2023 despite 2022 having 5x fewer samples — large 2022 values are over-represented relative to sample size, the signature of selection on noise. topo_aspect_sin/pH at 22=+0.825 from 18 fields is implausibly high for a single terrain derivative and screams small-n overfit.
    - *Test:* Permutation/bootstrap test: shuffle the property labels within each year and recompute the rate of |rho|>=0.5 in the 174-sample year. Predicted: the null already produces many large spurious 2022 correlations, and the observed 2022 magnitude distribution for unstable features is not separable from this null. Also: features with the largest |rho_2022| should show the strongest shrinkage toward 0 (and sign instability) when 2022 is split-half cross-validated.
- **[H4] (medium)** Among LABILE targets (NO3 200 labile rows; NO3 is the single largest unstable target at 123, S at 77), the unstable features are dominated by multi-season temporal statistics — ts_s2_MSI_std/NO3 (22=+0.55, 23=-0.191), ts_s2_NBR_std/NO3 (22=+0.482, 23=-0.229), ts_s2_MSI_cv/NO3 (22=+0.492, 23=-0.197), ts_s2_IRECI_cv/NO3 (22=+0.369, 23=-0.272), plus visible/red bands for S (l8_SR_B4_spring/S 22=+0.317, 23=-0.36). Temporal features are the #1 group in unstable (156 of 404).
    - *Mechanism:* GENUINE (not purely artefactual) year-to-year non-stationarity for labile properties, compounded by the confound. NO3 is mobile (mineralization/leaching/uptake shift it within weeks) and S has no diagnostic 400–2500 nm absorption and is driven by fertilization history + atmospheric deposition; neither has a stable optical fingerprint. The image-property relationship is therefore mediated by transient crop/moisture state, which differs between the 2022 and 2023 growing seasons (different weather, different fields/management). So even WITHOUT the spatial confound the sign could legitimately change. Here it is impossible to separate genuine temporal non-transferability from the YEAR×LOCATION confound because the two are perfectly aliased.
    - *Evidence:* NO3 and S together make up 200/404 unstable rows. The unstable NO3 features are temporal variability metrics (std/cv of MSI, NBR, IRECI) — exactly the quantities that track transient moisture/vegetation, not soil nitrate directly. For S, the unstable features are bare visible bands (B3/B4) with no sulfate absorption mechanism, consistent with a spurious, regime-dependent proxy rather than a causal optical link.
    - *Test:* Within the data-rich 2023 year alone (911 samples, 15 farms), split into two temporal/seasonal halves (spring vs autumn campaigns) holding LOCATION roughly fixed and recompute rho for these NO3/S features. If signs flip across seasons even within one year and one location set, the instability is genuinely temporal (lability-driven), not just the between-year spatial confound. Contrast with static-covariate/stable-property flips (H1), which should NOT reappear under within-year temporal splitting.
- **[H5] (medium)** Several unstable features for STABLE properties are short-window or single-season spectral measures whose VALUE genuinely differs between 2022 and 2023 imagery — s2_B2_late_summer/pH (22=-0.474, 23=+0.561), l8_SR_B3_summer/pH (22=-0.658, 23=+0.338), delta_spectral_BSI_spring_to_summer/K2O (22=-0.378, 23=+0.418), ts_spectral_BSI_std/K2O (22=+0.447, 23=-0.342). Here the target (pH, K2O) is stable but the FEATURE is a season/year-specific surface-reflectance state.
    - *Mechanism:* Acquisition/phenology mismatch rather than soil change. Unlike H1's static terrain covariates, single-date reflectance and 'delta'/'std' composites depend on the specific dates, cloud/atmospheric state, crop cover and soil moisture of each year's image stack. With 2022 and 2023 imaged in different years over different fields, the bare-soil vs canopy mixing and BRDF/illumination differ, so the spectral feature's relationship to a fixed soil property is set by the scene, not the soil. The sign of the proxy correlation then tracks which surface condition dominated each year — flipping when the dominant condition (e.g., bare vs vegetated, dry vs moist) differs between the two disjoint campaigns.
    - *Evidence:* l8_SR_B3_summer/pH flips from -0.658 (2022) to +0.338 (2023) for a property (pH-KCl) that is essentially fixed over one year — so the change must be in the IMAGE, not the soil. delta_* and ts_*_std features (change/variability metrics) are over-represented in unstable (Temporal 156, Spectral-composite 84) and are exactly the constructions most sensitive to per-year acquisition geometry and phenenology; their block_within stays near zero (e.g., s2_B2_late_summer/pH within=0.009) showing no stable local soil signal underneath.
    - *Test:* For these single-season spectral features, regress the FEATURE itself (not the target) on year, controlling for field, on the matched/overlapping fields: predicted significant year effect in the feature value distribution. Then, after standardizing each feature within-year (removing the per-year offset/scale), recompute the property correlation — if the standardized correlation stabilizes (sign no longer flips), the instability was driven by acquisition/phenology shift, not soil. Stable-property + static-covariate flips (H1) should NOT be rescued by this within-year standardization.

## weak  —  lens: PEDOLOGICAL / RS-PHYSICS / AGRONOMIC
**Key takeaway:** The "weak" bin (n=2327, 76% of all feature x target rows) is dominated by two physically distinct failures, not one. (1) Most weak rows are genuinely flat everywhere: mean |rho_full|=0.117 and mean |within|=0.068, with only 0.7% reaching |rho_full|>=0.30 and 7.6% reaching |within|>=0.15. These are sensor-physics mismatches: the chosen band/index/texture/season carries no chromophore or biophysical channel coupled to that soil property over bare-to-vegetated chernozem (e.g. SWIR B11 vs nitrate, red-edge PCA vs pH). (2) A small conspicuous tail (the digest eye-catchers like topo_TWI/pH full=-0.444, spectral_PCA_2_spring/K2O full=-0.389) has real pooled rho but lands in weak only because it fails the year-consistency gate -- it is a confounded zonal gradient unmasked by the spatially disjoint two-year design (1778/2327 = 76% are year-inconsistent; 33 have rho_2022 missing). Property-specifically, labile S behaves exactly as pedology predicts: lowest mean |within| (0.041) of any target in the weak bin despite the second-highest mean |rho_full| (0.133) -- sulfate has no diagnostic 400-2500 nm absorption, so every S association is borrowed regional gradient with no surviving local signal. The weak category is the correct dustbin for both physically uncoupled feature/property pairs and zonal confounds the design correctly demoted.

- **[H1] (high)** PROPERTY-SPECIFIC to labile S and NO3 (not stable pH/SOC/K/P). S has 398 weak rows with the LOWEST mean |block_within| of any target (0.041) yet the second-HIGHEST mean |rho_full| (0.133). Digest S exemplars: spectral_B3_summer/s2_B3_summer (full=-0.34, within=-0.084, betw=-0.285, 22=-0.12, 23=-0.397) and ts_spectral_BSI_cv (full=0.298, within=0.096, betw=0.352). NO3 weak with SWIR: s2_B11_late_summer/NO3 (full=-0.333, within=-0.142, betw=-0.26, 22=-0.502, 23=-0.171).
    - *Mechanism:* RS physics: sulfate-S and nitrate-N are mobile anions with NO diagnostic electronic/vibrational absorption in the 400-2500 nm window of Sentinel-2/Landsat-8. They are not chromophores (unlike Fe-oxides ~480/900 nm, carbonates ~2330 nm, clay OH ~2200 nm, water ~1450/1940 nm, organic-matter VIS darkening). Any apparent correlation is mediated through a green/bare-soil band tracking biomass or cover that co-varies with the regional fertility/deposition gradient, not with the ion. Because S/NO3 are also temporally labile (weeks-scale mineralisation, leaching, uptake), a cross-season image does not even observe the same analyte state. Hence rho can be non-trivial REGIONALLY (betw up to ~0.35) but collapses LOCALLY (|within|<=0.10).
    - *Evidence:* S mean|within|=0.041 vs mean|rho_full|=0.133 (largest within-to-full gap of all targets); spectral_B3_summer/S within=-0.084 vs betw=-0.285; s2_B11_late_summer/NO3 within=-0.142 with a wild year split (22=-0.502 vs 23=-0.171). The signal lives in block_between and dies in block_within -- the fingerprint of a physically uncoupled, regionally confounded feature.
    - *Test:* Compute partial Spearman of S vs spectral_B3_summer after removing block (k-means) means in each year. Prediction: |partial r|<0.10 in both years, whereas the same within-residual test for a true chromophore link (SOC vs VIS darkening, or pH via carbonate band ~2330 nm) gives |within|>=0.15. Equivalently, lab S correlates near-zero with measured bare-soil albedo at matched moisture.

    - *Mechanism:* Study-design artefact crossed with pedology: 2022 (174 samples, 5 farms) and 2023 (911 samples, 15 farms) are spatially disjoint, so YEAR is confounded with LOCATION. A static topographic/zonal feature (TWI; a spring PCA dominated by regional soil-colour/relief gradient) can show respectable pooled rho and even a real within-block rho in the farm-rich year (2023) but cannot demonstrate same-sign association in 2022 (too few farms, or NaN). The classifier rightly refuses to call these generalizable: a relation seen in one geographic cluster of one year is indistinguishable from a spatial coincidence. These are 'unprovable signal', not 'no signal'.
    - *Evidence:* topo_TWI/pH has within=0.221 (ABOVE the 0.15 generalizable threshold) and full=-0.444 yet is weak purely because 22=nan; spectral_PCA_2_spring/K2O has within=-0.258 and full=-0.389 but 22=nan. Both clear the magnitude bars and fail only the year gate -- exactly the demotion mechanism.

Prediction: TWI/pH and PCA_2/K2O move OUT of weak (signs agree across years) once 2022 has adequate farm coverage; randomly permuting the year label destroys their within signal, confirming the gate (not physics) caused the demotion.

A property's true fingerprint is a minor high-order PC, diluted away; the leading PCs then correlate erratically, sometimes with opposite signs at the two scales -- the signature of a feature reading different physics within vs between fields.
    - *Evidence:* spectral_PCA_2_spring/P2O5 has OPPOSITE-sign within and between (-0.216 vs +0.337); spectral_PCA_1_spring/NO3 has within=-0.202 but betw essentially zero (-0.011), a purely local wobble with no zonal anchor. Prediction: these targeted features achieve higher and sign-stable |block_within| for their matched stable property than PCA_1/PCA_2 do, moving pH/K out of weak; PCA features for labile NO3/S stay weak (no fingerprint to recover).
- **[H4] (medium)** Applies to buffered nutrient pools (P2O5, K2O, pH); weaker for SOC. - **[H5] (medium)** Property-specific by phenology: stable SOC benefits most from correct timing; labile NO3 may still fail.     - *Evidence:* EVI_spring/SOC: betw=-0.527 (strong regional greening gradient) but within=+0.097 (no field link); GNDVI_spring/NO3 within=-0.074 with a ~6x year swing (22=-0.566 -> 23=-0.099). The vegetation signal is real but lives at the regional scale and/or wrong phenological moment.
    - *Test:* Recompute the same VI at the peak-biomass window (late June-July) instead of spring. Prediction: within-field correlation of GNDVI/EVI with SOC (and to a lesser extent NO3) rises relative to the spring version; spring VIs stay weak. If even peak-season VIs stay weak for NO3, that reinforces H1 (labile mismatch dominates).
- **[H6] (low)** Not lability-specific; a resolution limit hurting any sub-grid (within-farm) property. Coarse/static covariates mostly weak: only 14 Climatic and 35 Topographic rows fall in weak. topo_DEM/P2O5 (full=-0.29, within=0.143, betw=-0.44, 22=-0.363, 23=-0.18); topo_TWI weak for SOC/NO3/K/S/P (e.g. TWI/SOC within=-0.17, TWI/NO3 within=0.011).
    - *Mechanism:* Pedological scale mismatch: ERA5-Land (~9 km) and SoilGrids (250 m) cannot resolve within-farm variation; they encode only the broad climatic/relief zonation governing soil formation across the steppe transect (chernozem-to-kastanozem gradient). For most targets the regional gradient sits in block_between (DEM/P2O5 betw=-0.44) while within is near zero (TWI/NO3 within=0.011). Where a property is not primarily zonally controlled at the regional scale, even block_between is small, leaving the feature weak everywhere. Coarse covariates are structurally incapable of carrying local, year-stable signal.
    - *Evidence:* topo_DEM/P2O5: betw=-0.44 but within=0.143 with a 2x year split (22=-0.363 vs 23=-0.18); topo_TWI is weak for 5 of 6 targets with |within|<=0.22 and frequent NaN/year-instability. Climatic features almost never survive into weak as exploitable -- they are either captured as zonal_only or vanish.
    - *Test:* Variance-partition each target into between-farm vs within-farm components (ICC). Prediction: targets/features where coarse covariates are weak have low between-farm ICC for that property, or the covariate's spatial scale exceeds the between-farm gradient. Specifically each coarse feature's |block_within| should be statistically indistinguishable from zero (bootstrap CI crossing 0) across all six targets.

## weak  —  lens: STATISTICAL / SAMPLING-DESIGN / SPATIAL: reasoning from the 2022x location confound, small-sample winner's curse, spatial pseudoreplication, support/scale mismatch, Simpson sign reversals, and the within-block vs between-block decomposition.
**Key takeaway:** The "weak" bin (2327/3072 = 76% of all feature-target pairs) is dominated by statistical/design artefacts rather than a single physical cause. Three design facts drive most of it: (1) the tiny, spatially-clustered 2022 sample (174 obs, 5 farms) inflates correlations by small-N winner's curse - median|rho_2022|=0.236 vs |rho_2023|=0.123, and 881 vs 139 features clear |rho|>=0.3 - so strong 2022 rho collapse in 2023 and the feature is dumped as weak; (2) YEAR is confounded with LOCATION (disjoint farm sets), producing Simpson sign reversals (461 rows with a strong 2022 arm opposite to 2023) that null out the pooled correlation; (3) spatial pseudoreplication means most apparent signal lives between blocks not within - |within|<|between| in 79% of weak rows (median within 0.057 vs between 0.159), and coarse-support covariates (ERA5 9 km, SoilGrids 250 m, TWI/DEM, PCA composites) only ever resolve the regional gradient. On top of these artefacts sits a genuine-null component: labile S/NO3 have no mechanistic optical handle (S median|within|=0.034; only 48/512 S features reach |rho_full|>=0.3) and ~19% of weak rows are true triple-nulls expected from screening 512 features. Net: most "weak" features are not weakly-signalled-but-real; they are non-transferable design artefacts (H1-H4) plus honest negatives (H5-H6). The confound cannot be fully broken with the current data - testing H1/H2 needs cluster-bootstrap CIs and a year-as-block fixed effect, and ultimately re-sampling 2022 farms in 2023.

- **[H1_year_winnerscurse] (high)** Applies to the bulk of the 2327 weak rows, especially the 1054 with a between-year sign flip. The headline-but-failing subset is visible in features like s2_B11_late_summer/pH (rho_2022=-0.601, rho_2023=+0.05, full=-0.142), s2_EVI_spring/pH (r22=-0.563, r23=+0.055, full=-0.145), spectral_GNDVI_spring/SOC (r22=-0.694, r23=-0.03, full=-0.108) and spectral_B3_summer/K2O (r22=-0.624, r23=+0.209).
    - *Mechanism:* 2022 has only 174 samples in 5 farms/18 fields vs 2023's 911 in 15 disjoint farms. Spearman over a tiny, spatially clustered 2022 sample has huge sampling variance, so screening 512 features per target (3072 rows) guarantees many large 2022 rho by chance (winner's curse / multiple comparisons). These do not reproduce in the larger, independent 2023 set, the pooled rho is diluted toward zero, and the feature is binned weak. This is an artefact of unequal/under-powered per-year N, not a real covariate-soil link.
    - *Evidence:* For weak rows with both years present, median|rho_2022|=0.236 vs median|rho_2023|=0.123 (nearly 2x); 881 weak features reach |rho_2022|>=0.30 but only 139 reach |rho_2023|>=0.30. Among sign-flip weak rows, median max(|r22|,|r23|)=0.276 while median|rho_full|=0.066 - a strong single-year value collapsing to ~0 pooled.
    - *Test:* Bootstrap the 2022 Spearman CIs (n=174, cluster-bootstrap by field): the inflated 2022 rho should have CIs that comfortably include 0 and overlap the 2023 estimate. Subsampling 2023 down to 174 samples in 5 farms should reproduce 2022-magnitude spurious rho at a similar rate. If true, the weak/unstable boundary should largely dissolve once 2022 is down-weighted by its effective N.
- **[H2_simpson_yearxloc_confound] (high)** The 461 weak rows where |rho_2022|>0.3 but its sign is OPPOSITE to rho_2023. Canonical cases: spectral_B3_summer/K2O (r22=-0.624, r23=+0.209), glcm_glcm_nir_ent_spring/SOC (r22=+0.397, r23=-0.142), s2_B11_autumn/pH (r22=-0.388, r23=+0.255), s2_B12_autumn/pH (r22=-0.519, r23=+0.243).
    - *Mechanism:* YEAR and LOCATION are confounded (the two years are spatially disjoint, ~1 shared field). The 2022 farms and 2023 farms occupy different parts of the regional gradient, so a feature can track soil one way across the 2022 cloud and the opposite way across the 2023 cloud. Pooling two oppositely-sloped clusters yields a near-zero/contradictory pooled rho (Simpson's paradox driven by the design, not by the property). Because location is nested in year, the screen cannot tell genuine non-transferability from a spatial-cluster artefact.
    - *Evidence:* 1054/2294 weak rows (46%) flip sign between years; 461 do so with a strong (>0.3) 2022 arm. The same physical band flips direction by year (s2_B11_late_summer/pH: r22=-0.601 -> r23=+0.05; spectral_B3_summer/K2O r22=-0.624 -> r23=+0.209), which is implausible as real physics and points to two non-overlapping spatial supports.
    - *Test:* Regress soil ~ feature with a year (=location-block) fixed effect / random intercept: the within-year pooled slope should be near zero and the apparent pooled effect should be explained by between-cluster offsets. If you could re-sample even a handful of the 2022 farms in 2023 (breaking the confound on ~1 field is not enough), the sign should stabilise. Cannot be fully tested with current data because year and location are not separable.
- **[H3_within_block_collapse_pseudoreplication] (high)** ~79% of weak rows have |block_within|<|block_between|; the 290 weak rows with |between|>=0.30 AND |within|<0.10 are the clearest (e.g. s2_EVI_spring/K2O between=-0.513, within=+0.084; s2_EVI_spring/SOC between=-0.527, within=+0.097; spectral_GNDVI_spring/SOC between=-0.329, within=+0.019; spectral_PCA_4_spring/pH between=+0.351, within=-0.154).
    - *Mechanism:* Soil properties are spatially autocorrelated, so samples within a farm/block are pseudoreplicates. A feature's apparent rho_full is carried by ~30 block-mean ranks (few effective DoF), i.e. it tracks the regional/zonal gradient, not local pixel-to-soil variation. Removing block means (block_within) strips the regional component and the local correlation evaporates. Such features are real regional gradients but useless for prediction once spatial CV is applied - so they are (correctly) demoted, but only because they ALSO fail the year test they land in weak rather than zonal_only.
    - *Evidence:* Weak median|block_within|=0.057 vs median|block_between|=0.159 (between/within ratio 2.79); compare zonal_only (ratio 9.64) and generalizable (ratio 1.52, the only class where within survives). The between>within gap is universal across targets in weak: pH within 0.059/between 0.192, SOC 0.046/0.139, S 0.034/0.216.
    - *Test:* Compute Moran's I on residuals and the effective sample size (n_eff via spatial autocorrelation). The between-block rho should track the regional trend surface; partialling out coordinates (x,y splines) before correlating should drive |rho| for these features toward their |block_within| value (~0). Under farm-LOFO these features should contribute ~0 importance.
- **[H4_scale_support_mismatch_coarse_covariates] (medium)** Coarse/derived covariates that survive only as smoothed regional gradients and therefore fail the local+year test: topo_TWI/pH (full=-0.444, between=-0.458, within=+0.221, r22=NaN), topo_DEM/P2O5 (full=-0.290, between=-0.440, within=+0.143), spectral_PCA_1/2_spring (spatially-smooth scene PCs, between>>within), and broadly the Climatic group.
    - *Mechanism:* ERA5 (~9 km) and SoilGrids (250 m) support is far coarser than the soil sample support; PCA/temporal composites also low-pass the imagery. Coarse predictors only resolve the between-farm regional gradient, inflating block_between while carrying no within-block (field-scale) information. When that regional signal is also confounded with year/location (H2) or cannot be confirmed across years (single-year NaN, e.g. topo_TWI/pH has rho_2022=NaN), the feature cannot meet the generalizable bar and falls to weak.
    - *Evidence:* topo_TWI/pH has full=-0.444 and between=-0.458 but within only +0.221 and rho_2022=NaN (unconfirmable); topo_DEM/P2O5 between=-0.440 vs within=+0.143. 33 weak rows have rho_2022=NaN (single-year, untestable for consistency). Climatic group has the lowest weak share precisely because the few climate features that do correlate do so as pure regional gradients (and thus get caught as zonal/unstable, not weak) - only 14/24 climatic rows are weak vs 73-78% for spectral/texture.
    - *Test:* Block-cross-validate with coarse covariates included vs excluded: their marginal skill should be ~0 under farm-LOFO and positive only under field-LOFO (the leakage-prone split). Variogram range of TWI/DEM/ERA5 should exceed the within-farm sampling extent, confirming they cannot resolve field-scale variation.
- **[H5_labile_property_no_physical_signal] (medium)** Property-specific: labile NO3 and especially S have a depressed signal ceiling. S examples are uniformly low on every axis: spectral_B3_summer/S (within=-0.084, between=-0.285), ts_spectral_BSI_cv/S (within=0.096), ts_s2_NDMI_std/S (within=0.033); S median|block_within|=0.034 (lowest of all targets).
    - *Mechanism:* Sulfate has NO diagnostic absorption in 400-2500 nm and S is set by fertilisation history + atmospheric deposition, so optical/SAR/texture features have no mechanistic handle on it; NO3 is mobile (mineralisation/leaching/uptake change it within weeks) so a cross-season image observes a different chemical state than the lab value. The genuine local signal is therefore near the noise floor for these properties, producing a true-null (not merely artefactual) weak bin.
    - *Evidence:* S median|block_within|=0.034 vs stable pH 0.059, SOC 0.046; labile features have a lower full-rho ceiling (max|rho_full| labile=0.340 vs stable=0.444) and lower p90 (0.238 vs 0.227 similar but with thinner upper tail). Only 48/512 S feature-rows reach |rho_full|>=0.30 at all. The 446 truly-null core rows (all three |rho|<0.10, 19% of weak) are disproportionately the no-physics targets.
    - *Test:* For S/NO3, even an oracle spatial model (kriging on coordinates + known fertiliser records) should beat all remote-sensing features; partial correlations of S with any RS feature controlling for farm should stay <0.1. For NO3, restricting to same-window imagery (image date matched to sampling date) should not lift within-block rho - confirming the limit is the property's lability, not feature timing.
- **[H6_truly_null_core_random_noise] (high)** The 446-row truly-null core (|rho_full|, |block_within|, |block_between| all <0.10) - e.g. topo_TWI/NO3 (full=-0.000, within=+0.011, between=-0.016), glcm_glcm_nir_ent_spring/SOC (full=+0.041, within=+0.010, between=-0.055), s2_EVI_spring/P2O5 (full=-0.089, within=+0.018, between=+0.007).
    - *Mechanism:* With 512 features x 6 targets, most feature-target pairs are genuinely unrelated; these are the honest negatives where neither a local nor a regional nor a year-specific association exists. They are weak for the simplest reason - there is no signal, artefactual or genuine. Importantly, weakness is roughly uniform across feature families (Temporal 75%, Spectral-base 78%, Texture 73%, Topographic 73%), so weakness is driven by target/design, not by a particular (e.g. over-engineered texture) feature type.
    - *Evidence:* 446/2327 (19%) sit at the null on all three axes; weak share is essentially flat across groups (73-78%) and seasons (71-83%), so no feature family is preferentially rescued. Even the highest-engineering groups (Temporal n=1080, Texture n=336) are ~3/4 weak.
    - *Test:* A permutation null (shuffle soil values within farm) should reproduce the |rho| distribution of this core (KS test n.s.). These features should never enter a stability-selected model across resamples; their inclusion should not change LOFO error within noise.
